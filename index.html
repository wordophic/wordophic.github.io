Hygiene hypothesis

Because ... they trigger cellular pathways that regulate immunity affecting immune response. It has been observed that countries having higher levels of sanitation are more likely to have autoimmune diseases due to a lack of an activated immune system. Autoimmune disease is the condition when the body starts defending itself from its tissues called autoantibodies (not against the foreign particles).
It is well-known that pathogens stimulate the immune system. In normal conditions, helper T1 cells are activated to stimulate the production of cytokines against infectious pathogens. In contrast, when no pathogen is noticed, decreased levels of helper T1 cell production take place, and in return, helper T cells 2 are produced. Higher T2 levels further result in allergic disorders like asthma, food allergies, etc. But it is important to note that exposure to only some pathogens can stimulate the immune system, other pathogens can have a negative effect. Also, the human microbe (the microbes in and on us) play a significant role in immunity. Maintenance of PCOS

PCOS is a polycystic ovarian syndrome that every 7 out of 10 women are diagnosed with. It usually occurs in the reproductive years of the ... female in which small fluid-like vesicles develop, called cysts. Some of the symptoms women suffer during this period are; acne, excessive hair growth, and most importantly irregular periods. The prevention and maintenance include weight loss,as it can prevent insulin production, cholesterol disorders, hypertension, cardiac problems and type 2 diabetes. This hormonal endocrine disorder mismanages the hormonal balance. Excess of insulin due to insulin resistance in the body causes disruption in ovulation leading to abnormal menstrual cycle. Some other symptoms include excessive androgen levels, sleep apnea, and high stress levels. high blood pressure, oily skin, and dandruff or dark patches of skin, fatigue, female pattern balding, pelvic pain, depression and anxiety and decreased libido. Though, if left untreated it can lead to infertility, whereas women who conceive with PCOS encounter problems like miscarriage, premature birth and etc. As far as the treatment is concerned, there is no proper recovery other than the maintenance through some birth control pills, diabetes and fertility medications, and pills to control acne. But on a good note, diet plays a crucial role and dietary patterns should be followed strictly.

1. A low glycemic index (GI) diet: The body digests foods with a low GI more slowly, meaning they do not cause insulin levels to rise as much or as quickly as other foods, such as some carbohydrates. Foods in a low GI diet include whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, fruits, starchy vegetables, and other unprocessed, low-carbohydrate foods.
2. An anti-inflammatory diet: Anti-inflammatory foods, such as berries, fatty fish, leafy greens, and extra virgin olive oil, may reduce inflammation-related symptoms, such as fatigue.
3 The DASH diet: Doctors often recommend the Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension (DASH) diet to reduce the risk or impact of heart disease. It may also help manage PCOS symptoms. A DASH diet is rich in fish, poultry, fruits, and vegetables.

Avoid the intake of; refined carbohydrates, such as mass-produced pastries and white bread; fried foods, such as fast food; sugary beverages, such as sodas and energy drinks; processed meats, such as hot dogs, sausages; solid fats, including margarine, shortening, and lard; excess red meat, such as steaks, hamburgers, etc. Moreover, reducing stress through self-care practices, such as getting enough sleep, avoiding over-commitment, and making time to relax, can also help a person manage PCOS.
