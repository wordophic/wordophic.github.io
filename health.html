8 tricks to treat frozen shoulder

Have you ever experienced frozen shoulders? Well, medically it is referred Frozen joint syndrome - a condition in which range of motion is decreased. It can either be... caused due to inflammation, or capsule (a sack of tissue and blood vessels, surrounding an organ) problems known as adhesive capsulitis. In case of frozen shoulders, the pain is induced all over the shoulder which is characterized by stiffness and rigidity. As far as the treatment is concerned, the patient has various options to go for, like exercises, medications, or even surgery. In case of exercises, physiotherapy can assist your frozen parts to restore their capability of movement. Moreover, patient can also give a try to following tips or exercises:
1. Always warm-up the frozen parts before exercising.
2. Perform stretches like pendulum stretch, towel stretch, finger cross, cross-body reach, armpit stretch. Stretching exercises help to loosen up the scar tissue around the shoulder and improve your movement of the shoulder.
3. Reach across your chest to reach the opposite shoulder. Use your working arm as an assistant to the affected arm at the elbow. Do this 10-20 times per day.
4. Strengthening exercises include outward and inward rotations to help maintain muscle strength.
5. Gentle progressing range-of-motion exercises help to prevent frozen shoulder
6. Warm shower can also help you in this regard.
7. Deep tissue massage is good for severe disorders, yet it may be unbearable to a frozen shoulder in some cases.
8. Heating beat by Activeheat can also be used to soothe sore muscles and help relieve the pain and discomfort.
It is important to avoid harsh exercises to avoid reinjuring the muscles and your joints. Consult your doctor in case of a severe immobility problem.

How to improve your eyesight and focus?

People are gradually being aware of the eyes-exercises and have begun calling it a natural remedy to cure eyesight problems. If you are facing any problem of near-sightedness or far-sightedness, ... cataracts, or macular-degeneration due to aging, then you are probably going to benefit from the following exercises:
1) Focus on your point finger a few inches away from your eyes and gradually increase the distance. Remember to keep focusing, and bring that finger slowly towards your eyes. Do it 3 times at least to improve your focus.
2) Create a pinhole with your finger and focus the images through it. This exercise will help you to strengthen your concentration.
3) Focus on your thumb ten-inches away from your eyes for 15 seconds. Then instantly find an object 10-15 feet away and focus on it. Repeat it five times.
4) Trace an imaginary figure 8 with your eyes on the floor. Let the distance be 10 feet. Practice it 30 times, constantly.
5) To cope up with eye-sight problems, the 20-20-20 rule is going to be your savior. Stare/focus on an object every 20 minutes at 20 feet away for 20 seconds.
6) Perform eye yoga frequently. Sit straight with your shoulders off. And rotate your eyes in a clockwise and anticlockwise direction. Remember not to abandon focus. Focus on the image along your direction and continue within the loop.
7) Blink your eyes slowly every time as it keeps moist and permits eligibility to focus longer.
8) Try to write on the walls rotating your lens along the direction of alphabets.
9) Palming your eyes is another efficient tip to relax your eyes. Put your light palms of both hands on your eyes and press them cordially with a light thrust.

IBS and IBD

According to gastroenterologists, both diseases have similar symptoms, yet different mechanisms. Irritable Bowel Syndrome involves colon only, regardless... of ulcers and lesions. Whereas, IBD is a chronic condition. An IBS patient suffers from food poisoning, constipation, gastric issues, nausea, vomiting, cramps and diarrhea. Additionally, food, stress or illness can also be factors of IBS. On the flip side, people who suffer from Inflammatory Bowel Disease face symptoms like ulcers and bleeding in the rectal portion, blocked bowels, bloody stools, extreme weight loss and anemia. There is no exact cure for such diseases, but they can be controlled with antibiotics, change in life-style or sometimes surgery (in IBD). However, pain can be felt in the whole abdominal region. A doctor can diagnose the kind of disorder due to specific localization of pain. For instance, pain in the lower-left region identifies IBS. While, left-sided pains are a definitive cause of ulcerative colitis (IBD).
